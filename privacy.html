1. Introduction

FBIM ("we", "our", or "us") operates a Pinterest API integration application. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your information when you use our services.

2. Information We Collect

We collect the following types of information:

  • Pinterest Account Data: When you authorize our application, we access your Pinterest profile information, boards, and pins as permitted by your authorization.
  • API Usage Data: We collect data about how you use our API integration, including timestamps and activity logs.
  • Technical Data: We may collect information about your device, browser type, and IP address for security and functionality purposes.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use your information to:

  • Provide and maintain our Pinterest API integration services
  • Create, schedule, and publish pins on your behalf
  • Manage your boards and content
  • Analyze and improve our services
  • Ensure security and prevent abuse

4. Data Sharing

We do not sell or share your personal information with third parties except:

  • With Pinterest, as necessary to provide our services through their API
  • When required by law or to protect our rights
  • With your explicit consent

5. Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your data, including:

  • Encrypted data transmission
  • Secure storage of authentication tokens
  • Regular security audits
  • Access controls and monitoring

6. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access your personal data
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of your data
  • Revoke API authorization at any time through your Pinterest settings
  • Object to processing of your data

7. Data Retention

We retain your data only as long as necessary to provide our services or as required by law. You can request data deletion at any time by contacting us.

8. Compliance

Our service complies with:

  • Pinterest Developer Guidelines
  • Pinterest Terms of Service
  • General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
  • California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)
